The Spark

"During my volunteer work at Ayush Social Welfare Trust Medical Camp in Valia, I saw elderly patients staring blankly at lab reports. Terms like 'HbA1c' and 'LDL' meant nothing to them. That day I realized: medical info needs to be accessible in the language people actually speak, not just available in English jargon."

As a Computer Engineering student at Bharati Vidyapeeth University, I wanted to apply my AI/ML skills to solve a real problem affecting millions.

The Problem: In India, 60% of the population doesn't read English fluently, yet 90% of medical reports are in English. This creates a dangerous knowledge gap where patients take medications without understanding their true purpose.

  • Prescriptions with indecipherable drug names
  • Lab reports full of terrifying medical abbreviations
  • X-rays with zero visual explanation for the patient
